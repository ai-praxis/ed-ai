# External Grant Opportunities

Major funding opportunities for AI-enhanced education research and language learning technology development.

## Federal Funding Opportunities

### National Science Foundation (NSF)

#### Computer and Information Science and Engineering (CISE)
- **Program**: Cyber-Human Systems (CHS)
- **Funding Range**: $500K-$1.2M (3-4 years)
- **Deadline**: Typically September/October
- **Relevance**: Human-AI interaction in educational contexts

#### Education and Human Resources (EHR)
- **Program**: Improving Undergraduate STEM Education (IUSE)
- **Focus**: Educational technology innovation
- **Funding Range**: $150K-$600K
- **Relevance**: AI-enhanced pedagogy and learning outcomes

#### Emerging Frontiers in Research and Innovation (EFRI)
- **Program**: AI-augmented Learning
- **Funding Range**: $2M (4 years)
- **Deadline**: Annual competition
- **Relevance**: Cutting-edge AI applications in education

### Department of Education

#### Institute of Education Sciences (IES)
- **Program**: Education Innovation and Research (EIR)
- **Focus**: Scalable educational interventions
- **Funding Range**: $150K-$4M
- **Phases**: Development, Validation, Scale-up
- **Relevance**: Language learning technology evaluation

#### GAANN (Graduate Assistance in Areas of National Need)
- **Focus**: Advanced language learning and technology
- **Funding Range**: $200K/year (3-5 years)
- **Relevance**: Graduate student support for research assistance

### National Endowment for the Humanities (NEH)

#### Digital Humanities Advancement Grants
- **Funding Range**: $100K-$325K (1-3 years)
- **Focus**: Technology tools for humanities research and education
- **Deadline**: Typically January
- **Relevance**: Computational linguistics and cultural context integration

#### Humanities Connections
- **Focus**: Interdisciplinary curriculum development
- **Funding Range**: $75K-$100K
- **Relevance**: Connecting language learning with cultural studies

## Private Foundation Funding

### Spencer Foundation

#### Initiative on AI and Education
- **Launch**: Spring 2024
- **Focus Areas**:
  - AI and Learning (culturally relevant tools)
  - AI Policy (implementation in educational contexts)
  - AI Ethics and Justice (equity and access)
  - Transforming Educational Research (methodological innovation)

#### Grant Programs
- **Large Research Grants**: $125K-$1M
- **Vision Grants**: Up to $2M for transformative projects
- **Research-Practice Partnership**: $300K-$600K

## Strategic Funding Pathway

### Phase 1: Proof of Concept (2027-2028)
- **Reynolds Leave**: Internal funding for prototype development
- **Spencer Small Grant**: $50K-$75K for pilot study enhancement
- **NSF EAGER**: $300K for exploratory research

### Phase 2: Development and Validation (2028-2030)
- **Spencer Large Grant**: $500K-$1M for comprehensive evaluation
- **NSF IUSE**: $600K for educational effectiveness study
- **NEH Digital Humanities**: $325K for cultural context integration

### Phase 3: Scale and Impact (2030-2032)
- **Spencer Vision Grant**: $2M for national implementation
- **NSF EFRI**: $2M for next-generation AI learning systems
- **Gates Foundation**: $1M-$3M for underserved population access

### Success Factors

#### Competitive Advantages
- **Open-source approach**: Aligns with foundation priorities for broad impact
- **Privacy-preserving design**: Addresses growing concerns about educational data
- **Interdisciplinary team**: Combines linguistics, AI, and education expertise
- **Institutional support**: Strong WFU infrastructure and strategic alignment

#### Key Partnerships
- **Academic collaborations**: Multi-institutional research networks
- **Industry connections**: Open-source AI development communities
- **Educational practitioners**: K-12 and higher education implementation sites
- **International partners**: Global language learning research consortium
