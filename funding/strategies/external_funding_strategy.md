# External Funding Strategy: ITS4LL Project

**Project**: Intelligent Tutoring Systems for Language Learning  
**Strategic Timeline**: 2027-2030  
**Primary Goal**: Scale pilot results to national/international impact  
**Status**: Planning Phase

## Executive Summary

This document outlines a comprehensive external funding strategy leveraging ITS4LL pilot study results to secure major federal and private foundation support. The strategy positions the semester leave pilot as a crucial de-risking phase that provides compelling preliminary data for competitive grant applications.

## Strategic Framework

### Core Value Proposition

**For Federal Agencies**:
- Open-source alternative to proprietary educational AI platforms
- Privacy-preserving, institutionally-controlled deployment model
- Rigorous evaluation methodology with measurable learning outcomes
- Scalable framework for national educational technology adoption

**For Private Foundations**:
- Democratization of advanced educational technology
- Educational equity through elimination of subscription barriers
- International collaboration and knowledge sharing
- Ethical AI development with transparent methodology

### Competitive Advantages

1. **Preliminary Data**: Pilot study results provide compelling proof-of-concept
2. **Local Deployment**: Addresses growing privacy concerns in educational AI
3. **Open Source**: Aligns with agency priorities for broader impact and sustainability
4. **Institutional Infrastructure**: DEAC cluster demonstrates technical capability
5. **Interdisciplinary Expertise**: Unique combination of linguistics, AI, and pedagogy

## Primary Funding Targets

### NSF Improving Undergraduate STEM Education (IUSE)

**Program Overview**:
- **Focus**: Educational technology innovation improving STEM learning
- **Award Range**: $300,000-600,000 over 2-3 years
- **Application Deadline**: October annually
- **Success Rate**: ~20-25% for well-prepared applications

**Strategic Alignment**:
- **STEM Integration**: Language learning as gateway to international STEM collaboration
- **Evidence-Based**: Pilot study provides required preliminary effectiveness data
- **Broader Impact**: Open-source model ensures wide accessibility and adoption
- **Institutional Commitment**: WFU infrastructure investment demonstrates sustainability

**Proposal Focus**: "Scaling AI-Enhanced Language Learning: Open-Source Intelligent Tutoring for International STEM Collaboration"

**Key Components**:
- Multi-institutional deployment across 5-8 universities
- Comparative effectiveness study with 500+ participants
- Teacher training and adoption support framework
- International partnership development (Europe, Latin America)

**Budget Projection**: $450,000 over 3 years
- Personnel (50%): PI release time, postdoc, graduate students
- Equipment (15%): Computing infrastructure and deployment costs
- Travel (10%): Conference presentations, international collaboration
- Evaluation (15%): External evaluation and assessment instruments
- Indirect costs (20%): Institutional overhead

**Timeline for Submission**:
- **Spring 2028**: Proposal development based on pilot results
- **October 2028**: Full proposal submission
- **Spring 2029**: Award notification and project launch

## Risk Management

### Funding Competition Risks

**High Competition**: Federal programs typically have 15-25% success rates
- **Mitigation**: Multiple applications with differentiated focus areas
- **Backup**: Private foundation alternatives with higher success rates

**Political/Policy Changes**: Federal priorities may shift
- **Mitigation**: Diversified funding portfolio across agencies and foundations
- **Adaptability**: Framework applicable to multiple educational domains

**Preliminary Data Requirements**: Some programs require extensive prior results
- **Mitigation**: Pilot study specifically designed to provide necessary evidence
- **Progressive**: Build evidence base through smaller grants first

### Technical Development Risks

**Scalability Challenges**: System may not perform at scale
- **Mitigation**: Phased scaling approach with performance monitoring
- **Fallback**: Focus on institutional deployment rather than cloud-based scaling

**Competition from Commercial Platforms**: Proprietary solutions may advance rapidly
- **Mitigation**: Emphasize unique value of open-source, privacy-preserving approach
- **Differentiation**: Focus on pedagogical grounding and institutional control

### Partnership Risks

**Institutional Commitment**: Partner universities may not sustain engagement
- **Mitigation**: Clear partnership agreements with defined responsibilities
- **Incentives**: Resource sharing and joint publication opportunities

**International Collaboration**: Political tensions may affect partnerships
- **Mitigation**: Focus on academic and educational rather than political relationships
- **Flexibility**: Multiple potential partners in different regions

## Conclusion
